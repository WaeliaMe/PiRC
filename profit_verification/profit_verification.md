# Profit Verification Model

## Overview
Since the hybrid token model distributes rewards based on project profit,
a transparent and verifiable mechanism is required to confirm revenue sources.

## Profit Sources

Profits can originate from several ecosystem activities:

- Application transaction fees
- Marketplace service fees
- Digital product sales
- Subscription services
- Advertising revenue

These revenue streams represent the economic activity generated by the token project.

## Verification Layer

To maintain transparency, profits should be verifiable through a structured system:

1. **Project Revenue Generation**
   - Applications or services generate revenue through user activity.

2. **Revenue Reporting**
   - Revenue data is submitted through an oracle or verification service.

3. **On-Chain Recording**
   - Verified revenue data is recorded on-chain.

4. **Profit Distribution**
   - Smart contracts distribute profit allocations based on the model.

## Security Considerations

To prevent manipulation:

- Revenue reports may require multiple verification sources
- Governance oversight can audit suspicious reports
- Projects with fraudulent reporting may face penalties or removal from the ecosystem
